INTRODUCING QRYSTAL

Built on the Qrystalnet blockchain, Qrystal is a utility token designed to be used for making payments and fulfilling commitments in the Qrystalnet app and on all the other platforms that make up the Qrysytalnet ecosystem.

The immediate use-case for Qrystal is the Qrystalnet app, a De-Fi app that allows coin staking and P2P lending. The app uses a new, unique method for calculating a user's credit score: evaluating not just their financial history but also their work output history. Some of the data that will be used in this computation will be derived from Papyra, our blockchain-based registry.

Following closely on the heels of the flagship app will be: Qazar, an online marketplace and marketing network; and SoChan, a social media and streaming app. An urban development enterprise, Fontana, rounds out the list. Each of these platforms is a standalone product that will be enhanced in some way by one or more of the others. They are all connected by their common foundation in Qrystalnet and usage of Qrystal tokens.

One Crypto-Coin, Multiple Applications
A unique proof-of event consensus mechanism allows Qrystal to be implemented in multiple use-cases. This makes it possible for Qrystal to be used on all of our platforms even though each one provides a different type of service from the others. A BRIEF NOTE ON THE CONCEPT
OF THE CROWDSALE

The world's greatest economies were built by regular people. At any given point in time, billions of people contribute to the global economy – most of them as consumers; others as production staff, business proprietors or business backers.

The Role of Business Backers
For decades, business proprietors have depended on the stock market as a channel for raising operational capital. When a company first "goes public", it offers its shares to the public in an initial public offering (IPO). Subsequently, holders of those shares trade them in the stock market. Shareholders may sell their shares after short periods or hold onto ownership for long periods, even buying additional units of a consistently successful company's shares. This way, many of the world's largest companies stay flush with cash and so maintain high productivity levels, which is one of the factors that make for a robust economy.

From IPO to ICO/Crowdsale
Nowadays, tech companies often prefer to raise capital through an Initial Coin Offering (ICO) or Crowdsale*, which is an offering of a large quantity of a unique cryptocurrency or cryptographic token created by the company. In this way, companies like Ethereum, Neo, and Iota, to mention a few, have become big players in the global economy. ​

We have chosen to go the Crowdsale route and, this way, get our products out quickly and smoothly.

* An ICO and a Crowdsale are very similar.
